Ginataang Manok

Description

Ginaatang Manok is a Filipino chicken dish cooked in coconut milk (gata), resulting in a rich and creamy flavor. It often includes ingredients like ginger, garlic, onions, and chili for added depth and spice. Some variations include vegetables like papaya, chili leaves, or malunggay for extra nutrition. The dish is similar to *tinola* but has a distinct coconut-based broth, giving it a slightly sweet and savory taste. Ginaatang Manok is typically enjoyed with steamed rice, making it a comforting and hearty meal popular in many Filipino households.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 2 cups coconut milk (gata)
  • 1 cup coconut cream (kakang gata, optional for a richer sauce)
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cups water (or chicken broth)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis) or salt (to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2-3 pieces green chili (siling haba, optional for mild spice)
  • 1/2 green papaya or chayote, sliced (optional)
  • 1 cup malunggay leaves or chili leaves (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Saut√© the ginger, garlic, and onion until fragrant and soft.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Season with fish sauce (patis) or salt and black pepper. Stir well.
  4. Pour in the coconut milk (gata) and water (or chicken broth). Simmer for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally.If using papaya or chayote, add it to the pot and cook for another 5 minutes until soft.Pour in the coconut cream (kakang gata) and add the green chilies. Simmer for another 5 minutes until the sauce thickens.Stir in the malunggay or chili leaves, then turn off the heat.Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!
