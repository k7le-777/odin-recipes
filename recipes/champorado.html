Champorado

Description

Champorado is a traditional Filipino rice porridge made with glutinous rice and cocoa, giving it a rich, chocolatey flavor. It is sweetened with sugar and often served with a drizzle of condensed milk for added creaminess. The dish is typically enjoyed warm as a comforting breakfast or snack, sometimes paired with tuyo (dried salted fish) for a balance of sweet and salty flavors. Its origins trace back to the Spanish colonial period, inspired by Mexican champurrado but adapted with local ingredients. Champorado is a beloved dish that evokes nostalgia and warmth in Filipino households.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup glutinous rice (malagkit)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1 cup sugar
  • Condensed milk (for serving)

Steps

  1. In a pot, combine glutinous rice and water. Bring to a boil and simmer for about 20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed most of the water.
  2. In a small bowl, dissolve cocoa powder in 1/2 cup of water. Add the cocoa mixture to the pot and stir until well combined.
  3. Add sugar to the pot and continue to cook, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens to your desired consistency.
  4. Remove from heat and serve warm, drizzled with condensed milk.
